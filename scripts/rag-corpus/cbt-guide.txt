Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) is a structured, time-limited, evidence-based form of psychotherapy that helps people identify and change unhelpful thought patterns and behaviors. Core techniques include cognitive restructuring (challenging automatic negative thoughts), behavioral activation (scheduling rewarding activities when depressed), exposure (gradually facing feared situations for anxiety), and thought records. CBT is effective for anxiety, depression, PTSD, and insomnia. A typical course runs 8-20 sessions. Homework between sessions is essential: patients practice skills and log thoughts. When supporting someone who feels anxious, encourage slow breathing, name the feeling, and suggest one small actionable step rather than offering medical advice.
